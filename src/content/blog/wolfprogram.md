### My Process & Why I Applied

When I first read about the **Wolf Grad Program**, what stood out to me was how *Fora sees travel not just as logistics, but as empowerment and storytelling*.  
That resonates deeply with me. I’m someone who’s always believed that the way we **connect, host, and tell stories** can change how people see the world.

As a UC Berkeley student, I’ve spent the past few years working at the intersection of **technology, creativity, and community** from product internships at Microsoft to launching projects that empower African youth and document cultural heritage in Guinea.  

Through this video, I wanted to go beyond a résumé to show what travel and creativity mean to me in motion. The clips come from years of journeys across **Asia, Africa, Europe, and the Americas**, each moment reminding me how hospitality, curiosity, and empathy can bridge entire worlds.


### Why Fora & the Wolf Program

What excites me about **Fora** is its mission to **make travel entrepreneurship accessible** giving people the tools and confidence to curate experiences that are meaningful, inclusive, and community-driven.  
I see the Wolf Program as the perfect space to **learn from leaders in modern travel**, build cross-disciplinary skills, and contribute my global perspective and design-thinking mindset.

Fora’s emphasis on **creativity, collaboration, and personal growth** feels aligned with how I approach everything, whether it’s building a startup, documenting history, or mentoring others.  

This video is both a reflection and a preview of how I’d bring that same energy to the Wolf cohort: curious, creative, and always seeking to connect people and ideas.
